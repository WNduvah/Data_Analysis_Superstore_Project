# Data_Analysis_Superstore_Project
An analysis of the Superstore dataset was conducted to understand company revenue, product performance, and regional profitability.

##1. Executive Summary
An analysis of the Superstore dataset was conducted to understand company revenue, product performance, and regional profitability. The analysis shows that the Technology category generated the highest revenue and profit, with Phones being the most frequently sold product. Furniture recorded relatively high sales but lower profitability compared to other categories.
Regionally, the West generated the highest revenue and overall profit, while the South region experienced the lowest profitability, with several orders generating negative profit. High discounts were identified as a major factor reducing profitability in certain regions and product categories.
Sub-category analysis revealed that Copiers generated the highest sales and profits per sale, while categories such as Tables, Bookcases, and Supplies recorded negative profit margins in several transactions.

##2. Problem Statement / Objective
The objective of this analysis was to evaluate business performance using the Superstore sales dataset and answer key business questions related to revenue generation, product performance, and regional sales trends.
•	Total company sales revenue
•	Total company profit
•	Average order value
•	Highest performing product categories and sub-categories
•	Regional sales and profitability
•	Identification of underperforming products and regions
